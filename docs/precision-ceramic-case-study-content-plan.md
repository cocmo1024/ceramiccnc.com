# Precision Ceramic Case Study Content Plan

## Site Positioning

CERAMIC CNC should stay focused on precision industrial ceramic CNC machining, diamond grinding, lapping, micro-hole work, inspection planning, and RFQ review. It should not drift into ceramic additive manufacturing, pottery, generic material science, or guaranteed tolerance claims.

The strongest content path is application-specific RFQ cases. Each case should translate one real purchasing problem into material choice, process route, critical features, inspection evidence, and quotation inputs.

## Current Inventory

The site already covers these foundations:

- Precision ceramic machining overview, RFQ checklist, material selection, DFM, tolerances, surface finish, and green vs hard machining.
- Core materials: alumina, zirconia, silicon nitride, silicon carbide, aluminum nitride, Macor, and boron nitride.
- Semiconductor equipment: wafer handling, SiC components, end effectors, vacuum chucks, chamber rings, plasma insulators, AlN thermal management, nozzles, and high-purity systems.
- Feature-driven RFQs: micro-holes, thin-wall sleeves, lapped seal faces, vacuum chuck flatness, high-voltage insulation, pump and valve components, sensors and measurement devices.

This means new articles should avoid broad restatements such as "ceramic parts for semiconductor equipment" unless the page adds a new RFQ decision.

## Incremental Case Lane

The next case-study lane should focus on precision industrial ceramic machining examples that are narrow enough to avoid duplication:

1. Published: Ceramic fixture plates and locating pins for inspection automation.
2. Published: Alumina ceramic inspection fixtures for SiC power module assembly.
3. Silicon nitride wear rings and guide bushings for high-speed mechanisms.
4. SiC lapped wear rings for abrasive slurry pump sealing.
5. Alumina high-voltage feedthrough spacers for vacuum power hardware.
6. AlN thermal-insulating spacers for power module test sockets.
7. Zirconia ceramic plungers for metering pump assemblies.
8. Ceramic edge-chip criteria for semiconductor packaging and clean handling.
9. Ceramic guide rails and wear strips for automated production lines.
10. Macor prototype fixture to alumina production transition.
11. Ceramic optical mounts for laser and metrology equipment.
12. Alumina and zirconia locating pins for precision assembly nests.
13. SiC nozzle inserts for abrasive or corrosive fluid dosing.
14. Ceramic reference plates and datum blocks for metrology fixtures.
15. Thin-wall alumina insulating tubes for compact high-voltage assemblies.

## Publishing Rule

For long-term daily updates, use this sequence:

1. Check existing articles and topic hubs before writing.
2. Choose one application, one material route, and one acceptance problem.
3. Use public industry signals only to justify relevance, not as filler.
4. Add internal links to material, DFM, tolerance, surface finish, RFQ, and adjacent application pages.
5. Generate fresh visuals under `src/assets/images/ceramic/posts/`.
6. Do not publish prompt blocks, visual manifests, fake customer names, guaranteed tolerance claims, or universal feasibility language.
