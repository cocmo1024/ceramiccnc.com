## Product Family Map

### 1. Zirconia Ceramic Structural Parts

Public signals point to zirconia ceramic as a primary material. The likely value proposition is mechanical toughness relative to alumina, wear resistance, corrosion resistance, fine finish, and suitability for small precision structural components.

Website relevance:

- Zirconia pins, plungers, sleeves, bushings, valve elements, metering parts, and wear interfaces.
- Content should emphasize drawing review, ground surfaces, edge-chip control, roundness, coaxiality, and surface finish rather than generic "zirconia parts."

Supplier due-diligence questions:

- Which zirconia grade is used: 3Y-TZP, Mg-PSZ, black zirconia, or another grade?
- Can they provide density, hardness, flexural strength, and certificate records?
- What OD/ID roundness, cylindricity, and surface finish can they support after drawing review?
- Are plungers ground, honed, lapped, or polished, and how are they inspected?

### 2. Alumina Ceramic Wear and Insulation Parts

Alumina is another primary product keyword. Public product copy connects it with wear resistance. The company description also positions alumina structural parts for industrial use.

Website relevance:

- Alumina insulators, sleeves, bushings, plates, spacers, valve seats, wear pads, and dispensing-device ceramics.
- Content should avoid broad ceramic claims and instead explain purity/grade, fired state, blank route, functional faces, edge quality, and inspection evidence.

Supplier due-diligence questions:

- Which alumina purity grades are standard: 95%, 96%, 99%, 99.5%, or other?
- Are blanks pressed in-house, injection molded, externally sourced, or customer supplied?
- What surfaces are normally as-fired, ground, lapped, or polished?
- Can they provide material certificates and batch traceability for export orders?

### 3. Ceramic Plungers

Ceramic plunger appears as a primary product keyword. This is commercially valuable because it maps to metering pumps, dosing pumps, filling systems, chemical delivery, medical/food dosing, and wear/corrosion environments.

Website relevance:

- Strong application path for ceramic pump components, valve components, dosing systems, filling equipment, and fluid-control RFQs.
- Key features: OD roundness, straightness, surface finish, end-face flatness, chamfer control, paired fit, and seal-interface behavior.

Supplier due-diligence questions:

- Maximum/minimum plunger OD and length range?
- Supported surface finish and roundness after cylindrical grinding or polishing?
- Whether plungers are paired with seals, sleeves, or valve seats.
- Typical inspection methods: micrometer, roundness tester, CMM, surface roughness, optical edge inspection.

### 4. Ceramic Dispensing Valves

Ceramic dispensing valve appears in the public product listing and keywords, including a semiconductor-related positioning signal. This is useful for content around high-cleanliness fluid metering and precise dispense control.

Website relevance:

Supplier due-diligence questions:

- Can they meet particle, cleaning, packaging, and contamination-control requirements?

## Supplier Qualification Checklist

Ask before using this supplier for customer-facing programs:

1. Material grades and standard datasheets for zirconia and alumina.
2. Whether material certificates, density reports, or lot traceability are available.
3. Tolerance and surface finish ranges by feature type after drawing review.
4. Maximum/minimum sizes for plungers, sleeves, valve seats, plates, and small bores.
5. Supported processes: isostatic pressing, injection molding, sintering, CNC, ID/OD grinding, centerless grinding, honing, lapping, polishing, edge finishing.
6. Inspection assets: CMM, roundness, surface roughness, optical, flatness, microscopy, pin gauges, gauge blocks.
7. Whether they can support English drawings, GD&T, RoHS/REACH if required, export packaging, and export documentation.
8. Cleanliness and packaging level for semiconductor-adjacent or medical/food equipment parts.
9. Prototype MOQ, repeat-order process control, and first-article documentation.
10. Whether complete valve/pump assemblies are supplied or only ceramic internal parts.
